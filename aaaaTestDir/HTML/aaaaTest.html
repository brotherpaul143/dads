Entering LaTeX commands takes some work. Anything between dollar signs is considered to be a LaTeX expression and subject to substitution. All open curly brackets must be escaped (preceeded by a backslash) so they aren't misinterpreted as cross references. Subscripts (underline) and superscripts (caret) must be replaced by the HTML sub...end-sub and sup...end-sup. Less than and greater than must be entered with the HTML &lt; and &gt;, otherwise trying to replace them would mess up sup/end-sup.

You can surround phrases with "LaTeX-style doubled quotes" or "doublequotes" it doesn't matter: there is a hard-wired conversion from LaTeX-style doubled quotes into doublequotes. Putting "LaTeX-style doubled quotes" in math mode markers (paired dollar signs) should make them doublequotes, too.
